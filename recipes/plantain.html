Plantain

Description

A plantain is a starchy, fruit-like vegetable, related to bananas but firmer, longer, and typically cooked before eating. It is a staple food in many tropical regions and can be boiled, fried, or baked and is used in both savory and sweet dishes. Plantains are a good source of carbohydrates, fiber, vitamins, and minerals, such as potassium and vitamin C.

Ingredients

  • Ripe Plantains
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps

  1. Peel the platains:Cut off the ends and make a shallow, lengthwise slit along the skin. Peel the skin away using your fingers or a knife.
  2. Slice:Cut the peeled plantains diagonally into slices about 1/2-inch thick.
  3. Heat Oil:Pour enough oil (vegetable or canola) into a large skillet to cover the bottom by about 1/4 inch, and heat over medium heat.
  4. Fry:Carefully add the plantain slices in a single layer, in batches if needed to avoid overcrowding the pan. Fry for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown and caramelized.
  5. Drain:Remove the fried plantains with a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  6. Serve:Sprinkle with a little salt (optional) and serve immediately while warm.
